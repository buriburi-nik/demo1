Engineering Workshop

Engineering Workshop is a basic practical subject that helps students learn fundamental workshop skills. It gives hands-on training in the use of common tools, machines, and materials used in engineering work. The workshop includes practical work in areas such as fitting, carpentry, welding, smithy, sheet metal, plumbing, and electrical wiring. Students learn simple operations like measuring, marking, cutting, drilling, shaping, and joining of materials. Safety practices are taught as an important part of the workshop. Students learn the correct use of tools, safety equipment, and good workshop habits. This helps in preventing accidents and maintaining a clean working environment.

Main Objectives

  • To understand basic engineering processes for manufacturing and assembly.
  • To understand, identify, select and use various marking, measuring, and holding, striking and cutting tools and equipment’s
  • To understand and interpret job drawings, produce jobs, and inspect the job for specified dimensions
  • To understand the various types of wiring systems and acquire skills in house wiring
  • To understand, operate, control different machines and equipment’s adopting safety practices

Engineering Mechanics Lab

The Engineering Mechanics Lab provides practical exposure to the fundamental principles of mechanics through hands-on experiments. In this lab, students study forces, moments, equilibrium, friction, center of gravity, and mechanical properties of materials using standard apparatus. The lab helps bridge the gap between theory and practice, enhances analytical and problem-solving skills, and develops an understanding of real-world engineering applications.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand and apply fundamental principles of engineering mechanics through practical experiments.
  • Analyze forces, moments, equilibrium, friction, and CG using experimental methods.
  • Improve problem-solving and analytical abilities related to basic mechanical systems.
  • Prepare clear and systematic laboratory records and technical reports.
  • Work effectively in teams and follow proper laboratory practices and discipline.

Construction Materials Lab

The Construction Material Lab is very important for the diploma holders in Civil Engineering. The course material has been designed for the students to know the properties of the construction material as well as the strength of the material as per IS code of practice. Further, practical input has been given for augmenting the learning by the students.

Learning Outcomes

  • Use suitable construction materials.
  • Use relevant natural construction materials.
  • Use relevant artificial construction materials.
  • Identify relevant special type of construction materials.
  • Select and use of processed construction materials.

Surveying Lab

The Surveying Lab is very important for the diploma holders in Civil Engineering. The course material has been designed for the students to know the different instruments used for surveying. Further, practical input has been given for augmenting the learning by the students.

Learning Outcomes

  • Select the type of survey required for given situation.
  • Compute area of open field using chain, tape and cross staff.
  • Conduct traversing in the field using chain and compass.
  • Use levelling instruments to determine reduced level to prepare contour maps
  • Use digital planimeter to calculate the areas.

Mechanics of Materials Lab

Mechanics of Materials lab deals with the testing of material which are used in civil engineering field. Mechanics of materials lab focuses primarily contesting of mechanical properties of materials, analysis of stress, strain and evaluation of deformations. Therefore, it is an important basic subject for diploma students in Civil and Mechanical Engineering.

Learning Outcomes

  • Test different Civil engineering materials on Universal Testing Machine.
  • Analyze structural behavior of materials under various loading conditions.
  • Interpret shear force and bending moment diagrams for various types of beam sections and different loading conditions.
  • Determine bending and shear stresses in beams under different loading conditions.
  • Calculate flexural strength of different types of floor tiles.

Concrete Technology Lab

Concrete Technology Lab deals with the testing of concrete material. concrete technology lab focuses primarily on testing of mechanical properties of materials like cement, aggregate, sand. Therefore, it is an important basic subject for diploma students in Civil and Mechanical Engineering.

Learning Outcomes

  • Determine select the properties of cement, aggregate and water used in concrete.
  • Select the suitable material for structures.
  • Determine the compressive strength of hardened concrete.
  • Design the concrete mix for different grades.
  • Use of NDT equipment.

Geotechnical Engineering Lab

The knowledge and skills of Geo-Technical Engineering Lab is important as any other subject of Civil Engineering. The theory together with practices of this subject will definitely help the Practicing Civil Engineers in Construction Works, especially in the design and construction of building foundation.

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify types of rocks and sub soil strata of earth.
  • Interpret the physical properties of soil related to given construction activities.
  • Use the results of permeability and shear strength test for foundation analysis.
  • Interpret the soil bearing capacity results.
  • Compute optimum moisture content values for maximum dry density of soil through various tests.

Hydraulics Lab

The Civil Engineering profession is much concerned with subjects like water supply, Sanitary Engineering and Irrigation Engineering, which need a sound knowledge of Hydraulics. Therefore, hydraulics is a very important basic subject for students of civil engineering.

Learning Outcomes

  • Measure pressure and determine total hydrostatic pressure for different conditions.
  • Understand various parameters associated with fluid flow.
  • Determine head loss of fluid flow through pipes.
  • Find the fluid flow parameters in open channels.
  • Select relevant hydraulic pumps for different applications.

Water Resources Engineering Lab

The Civil Engineering profession is much concerned with subjects like water supply and Irrigation Engineering, which need a sound knowledge of water resource engineering. Therefore, water resources engineering lab is a very important basic subject for students of civil engineering.

Learning Outcomes

  • Calculate average rainfall, reservoir capacity and control levels.
  • Estimate crop water requirement and capacity of canal.
  • Draw labelled sketch of earthen dam, diversion headworks, cross drainage work, theoretical and practical profile of gravity dam.
  • Prepare model of irrigation structure and maintenance report.
  • Design canal section.

Transportation Engineering Lab

Airport Engineering, roadways and Railways, as specialized topics of Civil Engineering constitute important components of Transportation Engineering. Many–a-times diploma holders are involved in the construction and/or maintenance of airports, road, and railways. At the same time a state may not have many airports or the facility of road or railways. In such a case the subject may be offered hands on practices on different testing on road material.

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify the types of roads as per IRC recommendations.
  • Implement the geometrical design features of different highways.
  • Perform different tests on road materials.

Public Health & Engineering Lab

The public health engineering lab focuses on providing water supply and, in some cases, sewerage operations. The diploma civil engineers should have knowledge about the different techniques, challenges and need to improve the public health by expanding their activities in coordination with the public health engineering department.

Learning Outcomes

  • Perform various tests to assess quality of water.
  • Estimate dissolved solids as per BIS codes.
  • Measure BOD and COD of sewage sample.
  • Draw line diagram of water pipeline system for a locality.

Auto CAD Lab

The AutoCAD Laboratory is equipped with modern computer systems and CAD software to provide hands-on training in computer-aided drafting and design. The lab enables students to develop skills in 2D drafting and basic 3D modeling as per industry standards. It supports practical learning in engineering drawing, design visualization, and technical documentation. The laboratory helps students enhance accuracy, productivity, and professional competence, preparing them for careers in engineering, construction, and manufacturing sectors.

Learning Outcomes

  • Create and edit 2D engineering drawings using AutoCAD commands.
  • Apply appropriate dimensions, annotations, and layering techniques.
  • Develop simple 3D models and views for engineering components.
  • Interpret and modify technical drawings effectively.
  • Use CAD tools efficiently for real-world engineering/projects and design applications.
